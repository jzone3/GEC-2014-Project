Family Farms


A family farm is owned and operated by a single family. They produce crops above the sustenance level, with the marjority of the work coming from within the family (with the exception of seasonal labor).


Genetically modified organisms, in short GMO, are widely used in industrial farms. Studies by the American Academy of Environmental Medicine show that the modified DNA can transfer itself into the bacteria that are harmful to humans. In addition, massive collateral damage that causes nutritional deficiencies. Products from family farms are not genetically modified, thus providing healthier and safer produce for the community.

The use of antibiotics and pesticides in industrial farms leads to many negative consequences. For example, antibiotic resistant crops result from consistent use of antibiotics. Additionally, the improper use of antibiotics in industrial settings leads to antibiotic resistance in humans due to biological magnification. On family farms, pesticides and antibiotics are prohibited,resulting in healthier, fresher produce.
